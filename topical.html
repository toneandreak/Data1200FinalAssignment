Fake news and critical thinking in the digital era

Merriam-Webster defines fake as something that is not true, real, or genuine[1]

And I would define fake news as being information that is indeed false or misleading, but
real. Its most defining characteristic is emotional exploitation.

Fake news seeks to go viral by triggering us with fear, anger or joy, and to go viral you must
be prominent on social media, but why? On social media we can exclusively choose who we
want to interact with. Thus, creating communities with poeople that very likely have
harmonious views, ideas and interests with ourselves. People we can more easily identify with
than others, and likewise also trust.

An excellent recipe for going viral with a fake news story is indulging in a comminity's
interest and falsely encourage their dreams or nightmares. In mid-2016 close to the us
elections, journalist Craig Silverman [2] discovered that teenagers from a small city in
Macedonia earned money by sharing fake news on Facebook that were pro-Trump and anti Hilary [3]
